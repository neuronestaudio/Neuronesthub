02 — The Science of Sound

Your brain is always listening

Sound reaches deep into sensory and regulatory systems. Below are the primary user-facing mechanisms and the deeper neural pathways that mediate them.

Arousal regulation

Tempo, complexity, and rhythm shift activation levels.

Masking

Stable sound can cover distracting variability, improving continuity.

Attention load

High novelty and lyrical content compete with cognition; low-load textures don't.

Restoration

Nature-like, softly engaging soundscapes support attentional recovery.

06 — This Is Not Music. This Is Not Random Noise.

Intentional design, not ambient content

Noise types

White, Pink, Brown, Grey — rolloff and primary uses vary; choose by masking and comfort.

Isochronic vs Binaural

Isochronic pulses are overt; binaural requires headphones and is subtler.

07 — Practical Listening Guide

How to actually use sound as a tool

Volume

Aim for 40–55 dB for focus; below 70 dB for extended listening.

Duration

Focus: 25–90 min blocks. Sleep: 30–60 min. Calm: 10–30 min.

Headphones vs Speakers

Headphones for binaural; speakers for shared/sleep contexts.

Active vs Background

Active = intentional attention; background = masking/support.
